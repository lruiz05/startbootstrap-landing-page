Terra Digitalis

Aim & Scope

Terra Digitalis is a peer-reviewed international e-journal of maps. Its purpose is to provide a forum for the publication of thematic maps related with Earth Sciences and Geography, among other disciplines, to facilitate the online distribution of digital cartographic works, recognizing its value as scientific products.

Audience

The journal main audience are the Earth Sciences and Geography academic communities. Although it accepts contributions from any region of the world, studies focusing on Mexico and Latin America are especially welcomed.

Terra Digitalis

Peer Review

Maps and manuscripts submitted to the journal normally undergo single blind peer review; however, double blind review or open review can be requested.

Periodicity & Languages

The journal publishes two issues per year, in digital format only. Contributions can be either in English or Spanish.

Ethics

Terra Digitalis, edited by Centro de Geociencias, Instituto de Geografía, Instituto de Geología, Instituto de Geofísica and Centro de Investigaciones en Geografía Ambiental of the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México, adheres to the COPE ethic guidelines.

Open Acces Policy

TTerra Digitalis is an Open Access journal and its contents fall under an Attribution-NonCommercial ShareAlike 4.0 Internacional (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0) Creative Commons license.
